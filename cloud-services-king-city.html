Cloud and Microsoft 365 services built around the King City workday

Growers coordinating from the field, truckers running dispatch from a cab on US-101, processors who need records available at 4 a.m. before a shipment goes north — the South County workday does not happen at a desk. Microsoft 365 and Azure are the platform; the value is in how they're configured and hardened for your operation. Here's what that looks like in practice.

Microsoft 365 Setup & Hardening

A new or inherited Microsoft 365 tenant built with the security baselines most installs miss: phishing-resistant MFA, Conditional Access policies locked to known devices and locations, a real Secure Score, and legacy authentication disabled so there is no side door left open. King City growers and carriers are squarely targeted for account takeover — a hardened tenant is the first line of defense.

Email & File Migration

Move off on-prem Exchange, old hosting email, or Google Workspace to Microsoft 365 with every mailbox, calendar, shared folder, and contact validated before and after cutover. We plan migrations around your operation's schedule — not during shipping week, not the day before a big harvest push. No lost data, no Monday-morning chaos.

SharePoint, Teams & OneDrive

Ranch and field office file shares migrated out of the old shared drive into a SharePoint and Teams structure with permissions that actually reflect who needs to see what — without everyone having access to everything. See our guide to which tool to use when.

Field & Road Access

Email, delivery records, compliance documents, and dispatch sheets reachable from a phone in the field, a tablet in the cooler, or a laptop in the cab — on secured, managed devices. A South County operation spread across multiple sites should not depend on one office PC to function.

Off the Aging Server

That on-prem server in the corner of the ranch office running Windows Server 2012 is a harvest-season time bomb — hardware failure, no vendor support, no real backup. We move grower, processor, and dispatch data off aging hardware into Microsoft 365 and Azure, with immutable backup built in from day one.

Azure & Hybrid

Azure where it earns its place: secure remote access for field supervisors and drivers who need to reach an on-prem line-of-business app, hybrid identity when you are mid-migration, or a VPN replacement that works reliably on the spotty connections common outside town. Not bolted on for its own sake.

Cloud Backup & Identity

Microsoft does not back up your Microsoft 365 data the way most businesses assume — deleted emails and SharePoint files are gone after the retention window. We add immutable cloud backup so a ransomware-encrypted or accidentally deleted file is recoverable, plus Entra ID and Conditional Access management so identity stays locked down after the initial setup.

Microsoft 365 for the King City economy

Growers and ranchers want records, maps, and email reachable from the field, not chained to an office PC. Food processors want line-of-business and compliance data backed up and available, not trapped on one fragile server. Trucking operations want dispatch and documents on the road. Microsoft 365 and a clean cloud migration deliver that reach and resilience — when the environment is built and hardened correctly.

The common thread is that Microsoft 365 is powerful but insecure by default. The value is in the configuration — the part most setups skip and the part we get right.

Cutovers planned around the harvest calendar, not ours

King City businesses don't put off a cloud migration because they don't want it — they put it off because no one has offered a plan that fits when a cooler can't go down or a dispatch system can't drop. We build the migration around your calendar: mailboxes and files are pre-staged and delta-synced before the cutover weekend, the final switch happens at a time you pick, and we validate shared mailboxes, calendar delegation, and line-of-business integrations before your team logs in Monday. If something doesn't work, we are on the phone — not waiting for a ticket to be triaged.

The same team manages the environment afterward. There is no project handoff to a stranger who never saw the migration notes. The engineer who ran the cutover is the one you call if email behaves strangely three months later. Cloud work lives inside managed IT and a real help desk that knows your environment.
