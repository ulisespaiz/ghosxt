Microsoft 365 built for Marina from day one — not patched together after the fact

Marina's growth pattern is specific: a company signs a lease in the Imjin corridor or off Reservation Road, hires its first five or ten people, and sets up Microsoft 365 by clicking through the wizard as fast as possible. A year later, MFA is still optional for half the team, SharePoint permissions are a mess, and a vendor audit just flagged the tenant as insecure. The platform is fine — the setup is the problem. We build or rebuild the tenant so it handles today's team and tomorrow's headcount without a rip-and-replace.

Microsoft 365 Setup & Hardening

Email, Teams, SharePoint, and OneDrive provisioned with the security baselines most rushed setups skip: phishing-resistant MFA enforced for every account, legacy auth protocols closed, Conditional Access scoped to device and location. For a Marina company starting fresh or fixing what was stood up at employee three, this is the foundation everything else runs on.

Email & File Migration

Moving from on-prem Exchange, Google Workspace, or cPanel-hosted email to Microsoft 365 — mailboxes, calendars, contacts, and shared file shares pre-staged and validated in parallel before the cutover. Marina businesses do not get a weekend of downtime; the team logs in Monday morning and everything they need is where they expect it.

SharePoint, Teams & OneDrive

A Marina team at five people can get away with a flat OneDrive. At fifteen people — after a few new hires, a contractor, and a shared-files nightmare — it breaks down fast. We build SharePoint and Teams channel architecture around the actual org structure: how project teams share work, how departments separate sensitive files, and how new people get access without an IT ticket every time. See our guide to which tool to use when.

Scalable Cloud-First Foundation

Identity in Entra ID, device enrollment in Intune, and file architecture in SharePoint — set up so a Marina startup going from five to fifty does not hit a wall at twenty. New-office buildouts in the Dunes or along Reservation Road provision cleanly into an existing tenant, not a new silo.

Hybrid & CSUMB-Adjacent Workforce

Ag-tech field crews, SaaS teams working partially from CSUMB, healthcare staff moving between offices — Marina workforces are hybrid by default. We configure Conditional Access and Intune so managed devices get clean access to email and files from anywhere, and unmanaged personal devices are blocked without a whitelist battle every other week.

Azure & Hybrid Infrastructure

Azure where it earns its place: hosting a field-data ingestion pipeline for an ag-tech team, running a line-of-business app that needs a cloud VM, or federating identity between an on-prem directory and Entra ID. We do not oversell Azure where a simpler Microsoft 365 license handles the job, and we do not undersell it where it genuinely fits.

Cloud Backup & Identity Governance

Microsoft 365 retention defaults are not a backup. Exchange Online, SharePoint, and Teams data need a separate immutable backup with tested restores. Alongside that, Entra ID Conditional Access and access reviews keep identity clean as Marina teams add people — so a departed employee or compromised account does not retain access that should have been cut months ago.

Why Marina businesses need the cloud configured, not just turned on

A tech or ag-tech startup landing its first enterprise customer, a dental practice moving into a new Marina building, a property management company adding three agents — these all share one pattern: Microsoft 365 was set up quickly and cheaply, and now it has security gaps, messy permissions, or an email configuration that failed a vendor review. The platform is fine; the setup is the problem.

Microsoft 365 is powerful but insecure out of the box. Multi-Factor Authentication is not on by default in most tenants. Legacy authentication protocols stay open unless someone closes them. SharePoint permissions sprawl the moment a second person joins. We configure all of it correctly the first time — or fix it if the first time already happened — so Marina businesses can grow on a foundation that holds.

Cutovers that do not cost Marina businesses a day of productivity

Most Marina companies put off a cloud migration because the last one they witnessed was the kind that breaks email over a weekend and leaves someone fighting with Outlook through Tuesday. We avoid that by pre-staging mailboxes and file shares in parallel — migrating in the background while the old system stays live — so the cutover itself is a short, planned event at a time your team chooses. Shared mailboxes, calendar permissions, distribution lists, and the line-of-business integrations your office depends on are tested in the new environment before a single user is cut over.

After the migration, the same engineer manages the tenant. There is no handoff document passed to someone who was not in the room — the person who built it is the one who answers when something breaks. The cloud work is embedded in managed IT and a real help desk, not treated as a one-time project with a warranty that expires.

The cloud and Microsoft 365 program here is a layer of the complete Marina IT services picture, not a separate engagement: it sits alongside cybersecurity, endpoint management, patching, and a real help desk in a single managed plan. As Marina teams scale and the Imjin and Dunes corridors keep adding businesses, the same foundation keeps working — no re-buy at the next hiring milestone.
