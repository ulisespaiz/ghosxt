Microsoft 365 and cloud services built for how PG businesses actually work

For a Pacific Grove inn, cafe, or professional office, the goal is the same: technology that works quietly in the background without becoming a project. Microsoft 365 can do that — but only when it is set up and hardened correctly, with the pieces your specific operation actually needs. Not the default configuration, not a template shared with a hundred other tenants.

Microsoft 365 Setup & Hardening

A tenant set up correctly from the first day: phishing-resistant MFA, Conditional Access policies, anti-spam tuned for a small PG operation, and security baselines that would survive an insurer's questionnaire. Microsoft ships a default configuration that works — it does not ship one that is secure. We close the gap before your first real email goes out.

Email & File Migration

Move from on-prem Exchange, Google Workspace, or a shared hosting account to Microsoft 365. For a PG inn or small office, Monday morning cannot start with missing email or a front desk that cannot pull up reservations. Mailboxes, calendars, and shared files are pre-staged and validated before the cutover; the switch itself happens on your schedule, typically over a weekend, and nothing goes live until it is confirmed clean.

SharePoint, Teams & OneDrive

Shared drives rebuilt as a sensible SharePoint and Teams structure — guest-communication folders separated from back-office records, permissions set so the right people can access files without emailing attachments around. See our guide to which to use when.

Hospitality M365

Email and booking-communication workflows set up for an inn, B&B, or cafe — with guest-facing mailboxes and shared inboxes separated from staff accounts and back-office records, so a seasonal hire does not have access to everything.

Quiet, Reliable Email

For the very small PG office or professional practice that just needs email and files to work reliably without a server in the closet — Microsoft 365 configured with security baselines and backups built in, so the technology disappears into the background the way it should.

Azure & Hybrid

Azure earns its place for specific PG scenarios: secure remote access for a multi-property inn, a booking or wellness platform that needs cloud hosting, or hybrid identity for an office that still has a local server and is not ready to cut the cord completely. We do not push Azure where it adds cost and complexity without a corresponding benefit — most very small PG businesses do not need it, and we will tell you that plainly.

Cloud Backup & Identity

Microsoft does not retain deleted email or files the way most small businesses assume — a guest booking confirmation deleted by accident, a shared calendar wiped by a departing employee, a malicious inbox rule that moves mail to trash. Immutable backup for your Microsoft 365 data closes that gap. Combined with Entra ID hardening and Conditional Access, the identity layer also holds up when a cyber-insurer asks for documentation at renewal.

Why Microsoft 365 fits the Pacific Grove economy — and where it needs work

Pacific Grove does not have large employer campuses or a downtown tech sector. Its economy runs on inns and B&Bs managing guest communication across a small staff, cafes handling point-of-sale and schedules, wellness and retreat businesses coordinating with contractors and clients, and professional offices — dental, real estate, financial services — where a single inbox carries sensitive client correspondence every day. Microsoft 365 is the right-sized platform for all of those. The problem is never the platform; it is the setup.

Default Microsoft 365 tenants have MFA disabled or set to SMS, no Conditional Access, open external sharing in SharePoint, and no backup retention beyond Microsoft's baseline recycle bin. Every small business that has been compromised through a cloud inbox — and there are many — was running a default setup. The configuration is the service; the license alone is not.

Cutovers that do not disrupt your guests or your week

Pacific Grove hospitality runs on small margins and personal service. A front desk that cannot access email or a reservation system on a Monday morning is not a tolerable inconvenience — it is a guest-facing problem in a town where reputation matters. That is why we do not treat the cutover as the finish line. Pre-staging happens before the switch date: mailboxes synced and verified, shared files in place, calendar access confirmed, booking-system integrations tested. The cutover itself happens on your schedule, and nothing is marked complete until every account holder has logged in and confirmed their access is clean.

Summer weekends and Butterfly Parade weekend are off-limits for cutovers unless you specifically request it — we know when PG is busy, and we plan around it. After the migration, the same engineer who ran it manages the environment. You do not get handed to a support queue; you get the person who knows your setup. The cloud work sits inside managed IT and a real help desk.

Cloud work that stays current — part of a complete PG IT program

A Microsoft 365 tenant configured once and left alone drifts. Seasonal staff means new accounts created in a hurry and old ones not deactivated. A new booking integration gets connected without a security review. Licenses accumulate for people who left. None of that gets caught when the cloud is managed separately from everything else. Our PG cloud work is part of the full Pacific Grove IT services picture: the same team handles cybersecurity, patching, and a real help desk, so the tenant stays current rather than installed and forgotten after the first invoice.
