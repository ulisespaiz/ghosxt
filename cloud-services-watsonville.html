Why Watsonville ag and food businesses move to the cloud

The Pajaro Valley runs on berries, vegetables, food processing, cold storage, and distribution — operations with multiple sites, heavy seasonal hiring, and field and warehouse staff who need access away from a desk. On-prem servers fight all three. The cloud fits them: a seasonal crew is provisioned and removed cleanly, a field manager works from a phone, and a second cooler or office shares the same files without a fragile VPN back to one building.

It also ends the server-replacement treadmill and the buyer-questionnaire scramble — national grocery and food buyers increasingly want proof your data and systems are secure, and a properly configured Microsoft 365 tenant is a big part of that answer.

Migrations without losing a workday

The reason businesses put off a cloud move is fear of the cutover — the weekend where email breaks and Monday is chaos. We plan around that. Mailboxes and files are pre-staged and synced, the cutover happens on your schedule, and we validate everything before your team logs in Monday morning. Permissions, shared mailboxes, calendar sharing, and line-of-business integrations are tested, not assumed.

Because the same team manages the environment afterward, there is no handoff to a stranger — the engineer who moved you is the one who answers when you have a question. The cloud work sits inside managed IT and a real help desk.

FAQs about cloud and Microsoft 365 in Watsonville

Do you provide Microsoft 365 support for Watsonville businesses?

We still run an aging on-prem server. Can you move us off it?
Yes, and it is one of the most common projects we do for Watsonville ag, food-processing, and cold-storage businesses.
