The Gilroy threat picture

Logistics and distribution businesses are targeted precisely because downtime is so expensive — a warehouse management system or dispatch board held hostage costs money by the hour, which is what ransomware operators count on. Food processors add operational technology and cold-chain systems that were never designed to be on the internet. Retail and outlets process card data at volume. And every one of them runs on email that attackers probe for invoice and payment fraud.

The defenses are the same proven controls in each case — EDR, hardened identity, immutable backup, network segmentation, and a human watching. What changes is the cost of being wrong, and for a business that runs on uptime, that cost is steep.

Six controls every Gilroy business needs in place

The Garlic Capital runs on tight margins and tight schedules. Whether freight is moving through a warehouse off the 101 or garlic is on the processing line, a security gap that stops operations costs real money fast. These are the controls that close the gaps ransomware crews and invoice fraudsters exploit — applied with the specifics of Gilroy's logistics, agriculture, and retail economy in mind.

Endpoint Detection & Response

Huntress EDR with a 24/7 SOC watches every endpoint — the dispatch PC, the warehouse manager's laptop, the back-office workstation at the Outlets — and isolates malicious behavior before it reaches other machines. For a Gilroy distribution business, the difference between contained and spread can be one shift's worth of freight that does not move.

Identity & MFA

Logistics email is a constant target for invoice and payment fraud — an attacker who controls a dispatcher's inbox can redirect wire transfers before anyone notices. Phishing-resistant MFA and Conditional Access close that door, legacy authentication is disabled, and shared accounts are replaced with individual ones that can actually be audited. See identity hardening.

Logistics Uptime & Ransomware

A ransomware hit on a 3PL or freight operation is not an IT problem — it is a contract problem. Carriers cannot report loads, dispatchers cannot book freight, and customers find alternatives fast. We tune EDR alerts and network segmentation so freight software and dispatch boards are isolated from whatever an infected PC can reach. See our trucking and logistics IT program.

OT & Network Segmentation

Cold-chain controllers, processing-line PLCs, and warehouse automation equipment belong on a separate segment, unreachable from the general office network. A malware outbreak on a Windows PC stays on that PC — it does not shut down the refrigeration, halt the line, or contaminate the network that runs the growers' field equipment. See our manufacturing and industrial IT work.

Immutable Backup

Ransomware operators look for backups first and delete them before they execute. Our backups are immutable and stored off the production network — the malware cannot reach them. When a grower's accounting server or a processor's ERP gets hit, recovery is measured in hours with a tested restore plan, not in days of negotiating with an attacker. See backup & DR.

24/7 Monitoring & Response

A warehouse that runs second and third shifts does not have the luxury of "we will check alerts in the morning." We pair EDR with a SOC that acts on detections around the clock, every day. A trigger at 11 p.m. on a Sunday gets contained before the Monday shift arrives — not discovered when the warehouse management system refuses to start. The speed of modern attacks makes this non-optional.
