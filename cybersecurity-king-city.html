The King City threat picture

Agriculture and food businesses are squarely in the crosshairs now: produce coolers, processing lines, and irrigation systems increasingly run on connected technology that was never built to be secured, and a ransomware hit during harvest can spoil product and stop shipments. Trucking and freight operations run on dispatch and ELD systems that cannot go dark. Ranches and growers run lean offices that are easy to phish for payment fraud.

The controls that stop this are the same ones used to protect government endpoints — EDR, hardened identity, immutable backup, and segmentation of operational technology from the office network. The only thing rural South County has lacked is a provider willing to show up, and that is the gap we close.

What we secure — built for South County, not borrowed from a city playbook

King City operations face threats that city-focused providers never think about: ransomware timed to harvest windows, invoice fraud aimed at ranches and produce brokers, freight-payment scams targeting truckers with thin margins, and OT systems on coolers and processing lines that were never designed with a network attacker in mind. The controls that stop this are the same endpoint and identity hardening used on government networks — not a consumer antivirus bundle dressed up with a managed-service sticker. We size and price them for a South County business without stripping out the parts that actually matter.

Endpoint Detection & Response

Huntress EDR with a 24/7 SOC watching every device — office workstations, ranch office PCs, and dispatch machines on the floor. Ransomware behavior is caught and isolated before it hops to the server or the cooler network. King City operations don't get a pass from threat actors just because they're rural.

Identity & MFA

Phishing-resistant MFA and Conditional Access shut down the account-takeover path that targets growers and small logistics carriers — wire fraud, fake invoice redirect, vendor impersonation. Legacy authentication gets disabled, not left open as a side door. See identity hardening.

Ag & Food-Processing OT

Coolers, processing lines, and irrigation controls segmented away from the office network and monitored, so a ransomware hit during harvest does not spoil product or stop shipments.

Trucking & Dispatch Security

EDR and immutable backup for dispatch and freight systems that cannot go dark, with email hardened against the invoice and payment fraud that targets logistics.

Immutable Backup

Air-gapped backups the production network cannot touch or delete — staged for fast restore during the times that hurt most: peak harvest, shipping deadlines, a Friday before a long weekend. Monthly tested restores against real recovery-time objectives for your operation, not a checkbox. A grower or carrier running on a failed restore assumption is not protected. See backup & DR.

24/7 Monitoring & Response

Detection without containment is just a log entry. When ransomware hits at 2 a.m. during an active harvest or before a big produce shipment rolls north on 101, we contain it that night — not after the morning standup. South County operations can't absorb a day of downtime the way a city office might. The speed of modern attacks means detection alone is not enough.
