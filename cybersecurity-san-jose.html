The San Jose threat picture

The South Bay concentrates exactly what attackers want. Technology and SaaS companies hold intellectual property and customer data, and a single compromised developer laptop or cloud account can expose source code, secrets, and customer environments. Their customers increasingly demand SOC 2 reports and pass-through security questionnaires before signing, so a security gap is now a sales blocker, not just a risk. Distribution, manufacturing, and professional-services firms hold financial and client data and run lean, which makes them attractive ransomware and business-email-compromise targets.

None of the defenses are exotic. The recurring problem is ownership — controls bought and never maintained, or never bought at all. That is the gap we close, with the discipline of a cleared engineer rather than a call-center MSP.

What we secure

Endpoint Detection & Response

Huntress EDR with a 24/7 SOC on every endpoint, layered with Microsoft Defender. Ransomware behavior is detected and isolated before it spreads.

Identity & MFA

Phishing-resistant MFA, Conditional Access, and the death of legacy auth — the single highest-leverage control on a small business network. See identity hardening.

SOC 2 Readiness

For tech and SaaS companies: the access controls, logging, change management, and vendor-risk evidence a SOC 2 audit expects — run day to day so the audit reflects reality and unblocks enterprise deals.

Immutable Backup

Backups the production network cannot reach or delete, with monthly tested restores — so a ransomware hit is a bad day, not a closed business. See backup & DR.

Email Security & Training

Email filtering plus security-awareness training, because most intrusions still start with a person being talked into something. Defends against the social-engineering attacks hitting SMBs now.

24/7 Monitoring & Response

Tooling plus a human who responds. When something fires at 2 a.m., it is contained — not waiting in a queue. The speed of modern attacks demands it.
