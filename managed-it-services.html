Managed IT Services for California Small Business

Most California small businesses still run IT the way they did in 2005. Something breaks, somebody gets called, the bill arrives a week later. That model worked when the worst-case outage was a printer jam. It does not work when the worst case is a ransomware crew sitting in your file shares for a month before they encrypt anything. Managed IT flips the relationship. You pay a fixed monthly fee, we keep the systems healthy, and the call you used to make at 11pm does not happen because the issue was caught at 9am. Ghosxt is run by a cleared DoD IT engineer with the same playbooks used to keep government endpoints safe. Built for California businesses that need serious IT and cybersecurity without hiring an internal IT team — whether you've outgrown a part-time IT cousin, lost your one IT person, or just want your stack to run at government-grade standards. What managed IT actually means

The phrase gets thrown around loosely. Here is the operational definition: a single provider takes responsibility for the day-to-day health of every user, every endpoint, every server, every network device, every cloud account, and every backup that your business runs on. That responsibility includes the boring work — patches, firmware, certificate renewals, license cleanup, expiring service accounts — and the visible work: someone responding when the new hire's mailbox stops working at 7am.

The break/fix model treats IT as a series of unrelated incidents. Managed IT treats it as one continuous system that requires maintenance to stay healthy. The difference shows up in the metrics. Break/fix shops measure how fast they can show up after something breaks. We measure how often you needed to call us at all. The first number we want to drive down is the second one.

What a typical week looks like on a Ghosxt-managed network

Monday morning, we open the dashboard for every client and check three things: any agent that went offline over the weekend, any backup that did not complete, any patch that did not apply. Anything red gets handled before lunch. Tuesday, patch deployment to non-critical workstations during low-load hours. Wednesday, Microsoft 365 license review and Secure Score check on every tenant — pruning unused accounts, re-tightening any policy that has drifted. Thursday, server-side and firewall patching during the maintenance window we agreed on with you. Friday, monthly metrics roll-up: tickets closed, patches deployed, threats blocked, backups verified, identities reviewed.

None of this is glamorous. None of it is supposed to be visible to you. The point is that it happens whether you remember to call us or not, which is the entire reason managed IT exists.

The enterprise stack we run on every client

We deploy the same class of tooling that Fortune 500 IT teams run, sized down without being dumbed down. The capability gap between this stack and the bargain-bin MSP loadout is enormous; the cost difference at our scale is small.

Endpoint Detection & Response

Huntress EDR on every endpoint, with 24/7 SOC monitoring and human analyst review. Layered with Microsoft Defender for Endpoint where the licensing supports it. Ransomware behavior detected and isolated before it spreads.

Identity & Conditional Access

Microsoft Entra ID with Conditional Access policies that block legacy auth, enforce phishing-resistant MFA, and lock down high-risk sign-ins. FIDO2 hardware keys for admins. The single highest-leverage control on a small business network.

Device Management

Microsoft Intune as the MDM and configuration plane. Every laptop, desktop, and phone you issue is enrolled, hardened, and remotely manageable from day one. Compliance policies enforced automatically.

Patch & Vulnerability Management

Automated patching for Windows, macOS, and 200+ third-party apps. Continuous vulnerability scanning. Critical CVEs fast-tracked. No more "is that machine still running an old build?" surprises.

Backup & Recovery

Veeam Data Platform or Datto SIRIS depending on your environment, paired with an immutable cloud target the production network cannot reach. Tested restores monthly. Detailed in our backup and disaster recovery service.

Network & Edge

UniFi Enterprise for switching and wireless, pfSense Plus, Sophos XGS, or Cisco Meraki on the edge depending on what your environment needs. Designed and documented under our network design service.

Virtualization & Cloud

Hyper-V or VMware vSphere on the server side. Microsoft 365, SharePoint, Teams, and Exchange Online managed in-house. Azure infrastructure when it earns its place. Full coverage in our cloud services.

RMM & Telemetry

Enterprise RMM agent on every Windows and Mac endpoint, with custom telemetry and alerting tuned per client. Automation does the boring work so the senior engineer is not also fielding password resets all day.

How Ghosxt is structured differently than the bigger MSPs

Most MSPs you would hire in California are organized like a call center. You file a ticket, it goes to a tier-1 technician — often offshore — who reads from a script. If they cannot resolve it, it escalates. The senior engineer who can actually solve your problem is two layers up and three days away. By the time you talk to them, you have explained the issue four times.

Ghosxt is the opposite shape. There is one cleared engineer who knows your environment, your team, your line-of-business apps, and your network. There is no tier-1. There is no offshore. There is no ticket queue you cannot see into. When you call, you get the engineer who built your environment. The discipline behind that is what makes the model viable: enterprise tooling, written documentation, and automation that does the boring work, so the senior engineer is not also resetting Outlook passwords.

Pricing is fixed, per user per month, with everything included. You see the same number every month. There are no surprise project invoices for things that should have been part of normal operations. Strategic planning sessions are quarterly at no additional cost — vCIO when you need vCIO, helpdesk when you need helpdesk.

FAQs about managed IT

How fast do you respond when something breaks?
Most issues are picked up by our monitoring before a user notices, and resolved remotely the same hour. For tickets you raise directly, business-hours response is under 15 minutes and after-hours emergencies are answered live by the engineer who built your environment, not a call center.
Do I have to sign a long contract?
Our standard agreement is 12 months at the listed per-user price. The term gives us time to properly engineer your environment and locks your rate — no price increases during your initial year. Early cancellation during the term costs 50% of the remaining months; after the first 12 months, cancel anytime with 30 days written notice and no fee. If you want zero term commitment from day one, month-to-month is available at +15%. You own your data and we'll export it on request.
Do you support Mac as well as Windows?
Yes. The full stack runs on macOS and Windows, and we manage iOS and Android phones through Microsoft Intune. Linux servers are also supported on managed plans.
We already have an internal IT person. Can you co-manage?
Yes. Co-managed IT is a common arrangement. We bring the enterprise tooling, the cybersecurity stack, and after-hours coverage; your internal IT keeps owning end-user relationships and project work. We document the boundary together.
What size business is this designed for?
There's no hard size limit. We support businesses ranging from tiny teams on the Tiny Team Managed Security plan up through 50+ employee operations on the per-user tiers. The right fit is less about headcount and more about whether you've outgrown the part-time-IT-cousin arrangement, lost your one IT person, or need your stack to operate at government-grade security standards. We serve clients on-site across Monterey County and the Central Coast, and remotely across the United States.
Are you actually available after hours?
Yes for emergencies. The Huntress SOC monitors endpoints around the clock and engages us automatically on a critical event. Direct after-hours contact for client escalations is included; routine helpdesk runs business hours Pacific.
