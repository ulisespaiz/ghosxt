Common IT challenges for Monterey businesses

Monterey is two economies in one place: a hospitality and tourism scene that lives and dies by uptime, and a defense and research community near NPS and DLI with serious compliance obligations. The IT problems reflect that split, and these are the ones we solve most.

  • Hospitality uptime and PCI. A hotel on Cannery Row or a restaurant downtown cannot run cards on a frozen terminal during a Friday rush. We keep PCI-compliant POS, property-management systems, and secure guest Wi-Fi reliable through peak season.
  • CMMC and DoD compliance. Defense contractors and subcontractors near NPS and DLI face NIST 800-171, DFARS, and CMMC requirements. As a cleared DoD engineer, we implement and document the controls correctly — not as a checkbox exercise.
  • Salt-air and coastal hardware failures. Corrosion quietly kills cheap network gear near the bay. We spec equipment that survives the environment and monitor it so failures are caught early.
  • Seasonal traffic spikes. Car Week, festivals, and the summer surge push guest networks and reservation systems to the limit. We size and segment networks so busy weekends stay smooth.
  • Bandwidth gaps. Connectivity thins out toward Pebble and the outer areas. We design with failover so one provider's hiccup does not take a business offline.
