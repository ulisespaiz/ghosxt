Salinas Microsoft 365 Support

Almost every Salinas business we work with runs on Microsoft 365: Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive, and the identity that sits underneath all of it in Entra ID. The platform is excellent. Most tenants are configured poorly out of habit. Legacy authentication is still on. MFA is partial. Conditional Access is empty. Admin accounts are shared with daily-driver accounts. Default sharing is wide open. The result is a tenant that looks fine until a credential is phished or a laptop is stolen, and then everything is open.

Ghosxt Microsoft 365 support in Salinas covers:

  • New tenant setup or migration from Google, on-prem Exchange, or another tenant
  • Baseline hardening: Conditional Access, Defender for Business, Intune, MFA enforcement, legacy auth disabled, admin separation
  • Entra ID identity, groups, and license assignment via groups
  • SharePoint and Teams structure, governance, and external sharing controls
  • Email security: DMARC, DKIM, SPF, anti-impersonation, attachment and link scanning
  • Mailbox migrations, shared mailboxes, distribution groups, calendar permissions
  • OneDrive rollout, sync hygiene, and known-folder redirection
  • Intune device policy for Windows, macOS, iOS, and Android
  • Microsoft 365 backup with a third-party immutable copy (Microsoft does not back up your tenant for you)
  • Licensing audit — we will tell you if you are paying for seats you no longer use
  • Day-to-day administration: new hires, departures, password resets, app permissions

Microsoft 365 baseline hardening is included in every Ghosxt managed IT tier, including the Tiny Team Managed Security plan. Standalone M365 projects are also available for businesses that just need the tenant fixed once. Salinas Backup and Disaster Recovery

Backups are the boring piece of IT that nobody thinks about until ransomware hits, a fileserver dies, lightning takes out a switch, someone permanently deletes the wrong folder in SharePoint, or a former employee logs in and starts deleting on the way out. We design and monitor backup systems for Salinas businesses so that the worst day of the year is a few hours of restore work, not a closed business and an insurance claim.

The Ghosxt Salinas backup and disaster recovery approach:

  • Microsoft 365 protected by a dedicated third-party backup (Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams)
  • On-prem and virtualized servers backed up image-level with bare-metal restore tested
  • Line-of-business databases (SQL, QuickBooks, produce ERPs, dispatch and TMS) with application-aware backup
  • Immutable cloud copies in a separate identity boundary, unreachable to a compromised admin
  • 3-2-1 strategy in practice: production, local, and immutable off-site copy
  • Backup health alerts that go to an engineer, not into a dashboard nobody opens
  • Documented restore testing on a real schedule, not "we ran a test once"
  • A one-page recovery plan that an operations manager can actually follow if the engineer is unavailable
  • Defined RPOs and RTOs that match your business, not the vendor brochure
  • PG&E PSPS and outage continuity planning built into the recovery story

What onboarding looks like for a Salinas business

Switching IT providers in Salinas is a documented project, not a chaotic handover. Here is how a typical onboarding runs, whether you are coming off an internal hire, a freelance IT contractor, or another MSP.

  • Week 0 — Salinas IT assessment. 30 to 60 minute call, then a walk-through of your environment. Walk away with a written punch list of what to fix first, regardless of whether you hire us.
  • Week 1 — Documentation and access. We inventory devices, accounts, Microsoft 365 tenant, line-of-business apps, network, and vendors. Credentials get transferred into a managed password vault. Admin access is split from daily-driver accounts.
  • Week 2 — Tooling deployment. Endpoint monitoring, EDR, patch management, and DNS filtering go onto every device. Backups are validated or rebuilt.

  • Week 4 — Documentation deliverable and handoff. Network diagram, asset register, M365 baseline document, backup and recovery plan, and a written 30-60-90 day forward plan in your hands.
  • Ongoing — Monthly reports and quarterly business reviews. You see what we did, what we found, what is next, and what your IT and security risk picture actually looks like.
