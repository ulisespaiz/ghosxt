The problem with a phone line tied to a building

An old on-premise phone system is a single point of failure: it goes down with the internet, the power, or the box in the closet nobody has serviced in years, and the business goes silent right when a customer is trying to reach you. Cloud VoIP moves the phone system off that box entirely — calls route through the internet to whatever device your team actually has in hand, and a local outage doesn't have to mean a missed call.

We've set this up across client environments where the main line couldn't afford to go dark — reservation desks, dispatch lines, front-office phones — and it's consistently one of the highest-value, lowest-drama moves a small business makes.

VoIP & Business Phone FAQs

Do we need new hardware for VoIP?
Usually not much. Most modern VoIP runs on softphone apps on the computers and phones your team already has, with physical desk phones optional for the roles that want one. We'll size the right mix for your team rather than defaulting to a phone on every desk.
What happens to our phones if the internet or power goes out?
We configure automatic failover so calls route to mobile phones or a backup number when your primary connection drops, so an outage doesn't mean your business goes silent. This is one of the most common reasons small businesses move off an old on-premise phone system.
Can we keep our existing phone number?
Yes. Number porting to a new VoIP provider is standard, and we handle the process and timing so there's no gap in service and no missed calls during the cutover.
Does VoIP work with Microsoft Teams?
Yes. Microsoft Teams Phone lets your team make and receive calls directly inside Teams, on the same app they already use for chat and meetings, instead of running a separate phone system alongside it. We set this up as part of a broader Microsoft 365 deployment when it's the right fit.
